Statistical Arbitrage

Background

Statistical Arbitrage (Stat Arb) represents 9% of AUM of quantitative strategies. Stat arb uses various mathematical techniques to identify the relationships between the movements of different instruments.


Statistical Arbitrage strategies benefit from discrepancies between predictions and the actual market. This is typically a shorter-term holding period strategy i.e. intraday to weeks. According to Aurum, Statistical arbitrage is the second most profitable quantitative strategy, and has the highest risk adjusted returns for any quantitative strategy. This makes it the ideal tool for us to improve the return generation of the Empros Fund.


Evidence

As highlighted by Avellaneda (2008) with his study on Stat Arb in the US equities market, mean reversion processes can generate low-risk signals. Due to its fundamentally low-risk nature, accounting for transaction costs and signal speed, Stat Arb generates notably high risk adjusted returns.

Building on Shah and Zhang (2014), Fischer (2018) was the first academic to highlight the opportunity of Stat Arb in the digital asset market, modelling his work around papers centred on the US equity market. Fisher concluded that, before fees, Stat Arb strategies were able to generate 0.15% a day (54.75% p.a.).

Figure 1 : Quantitative strategies ranked via return, Stat Arb generating over 10% p.a. as the 2nd most profitable quant strategy. . Source : Aurum insight


Implementation

Empros incorporates Statistical Arbitrage as part of the Empros Fund due to its consistent delivery of high risk adjusted returns, due to the inherent low risk nature of the strategies. In such a nascent market, such as the digital asset market, there is consistent and ample opportunity for arbitrage, in aspects, across exchanges, products, and derivatives.


Aside from statistical arbitrage, Empros leverages futures-to-cash as well as delta-one arbitrage to diversify the Empros Fund, and exploit market inefficiencies on a broad range of products, timeframes, and exchanges. Arbitrage works to balance out the risk of the Empros Fund, reducing fund exposure whilst still maintaining exposure to the underlying.
